<HEADLINE>
Urgent: Iraqi defense minister says Iraq strongly resists enemy
</HEADLINE>
<TEXT>
<P>
BAGHDAD, March 21 (Xinhua)
</P>
<P>
Iraqi Defence Minister Sultan Hashim Ahmed said here  Friday evening
that Iraqi forces are strongly resisting American and British
invadors.
</P>
<P>
At a press conference held in Baghdad, he acknowledged for the first
time that US and British troops have crossed the borders into Iraq
from Kuwait and attacked Um al-Qasser and headed towards Basra.
</P>
<P>
He also said that US and British paratroopers landed in a number of
border towns close with Syria and Jordan. Towns they attacked include
Rutba, 350 km west to Baghdad, al-Nakhaib near Mosul, besides Al-Walid
military base close to the Syrian border.
</P>
<P>
But the paratroopers met strong resistance from Iraqi troops and
tribesmen.
</P>
<P>
In Rutba area, "two enemy armed vehicles were destroyed by rocket
propelled grenades," he said, adding battles in that area still
continued.
</P>
<P>
The press conference was disrupted by loud explosions at around 2100
local time when coalition forces launched massive bombardment on
Baghdad.
</P>
<P>
He also said that British forces landed behind al-Saw peninsula and
bombarded the town of al Saw by artillery. "The enemy forces have
advanced further more into the northern Rumeila area, which has rich
oil reserve."
</P>
<P>
Another army force heads the city of Basra 520 km north of Baghdad,
but "was stopped because of strong resistance.
</P>
<P>
Meanwhile, a statement issued by the military said Iraqi missile force
fired surface-to-surface missiles to coalition forces concentrations
in Al-Rouqa, and Ali Salem base in Kuwait on Friday.
</P>
