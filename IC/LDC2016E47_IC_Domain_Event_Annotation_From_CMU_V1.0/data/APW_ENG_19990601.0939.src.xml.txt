<P>
Key dates in the fight by the Kurdistan Workers Party, or PKK, against
Turkey
</P>
<P>
Nov. 1978 _ Abdullah Ocalan forms the Marxist Kurdistan Workers Party,
the Partiya Karkeren Kurdistan, or PKK, in southeastern Turkey.
</P>
<P>
May 1979 _ Turkey detains several members of the outlawed group.
Ocalan flees to Syria a few days later.
</P>
<P>
Sept. 1980 _ Military stages coup, prominent PKK members flee abroad.
</P>
<P>
1982 _ PKK joins forces with other leftist groups under a short-lived
umbrella group, the United Resistance Front Against Fascism.
</P>
<P>
1984 _ PKK forms armed units, which fight under the name of the
Kurdistan Liberation Union.
</P>
<P>
Aug. 1984 _ PKK carries out its first armed attack, killing one
soldier.
</P>
<P>
1985 _ PKK's political wing, the Kurdistan National Liberation Front,
is formed during a meeting in northern Iraq.
</P>
<P>
1993 _ Lebanon bows to Turkish pressure and closes the PKK's main
training camp in the Bekaa Valley.
</P>
<P>
March 1993 _ PKK declares unilateral cease-fire. PKK fighters break
the cease-fire days later, killing 33 unarmed Turkish soldiers.
</P>
<P>
June 1994 _ PKK carries out its first bombing against tourist sites,
injuring 13 people, including seven foreign tourists, in the
Mediterranean resort town of Fethiye.
</P>
<P>
April 1995 _ 65-seat Kurdish Parliament in exile formed in The Hague,
the Netherlands.
</P>
<P>
June 1996 _ PKK stages its first suicide bombing, killing six soldiers
in the eastern city of Tunceli.
</P>
<P>
Feb. 15 1999 _ Ocalan captured in Kenya, sparking angry protests by
PKK sympathizers around the world.
</P>
<P>
May 31, 1999 _ Ocalan's trial on charges of treason, an offense
punishable by death, begins on the Turkish prison island of Imrali.
